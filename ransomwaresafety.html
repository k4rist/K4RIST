What is ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malicicious software that encrypts your data or locks out your device until you pay.
They usually tell you to pay 2 bitcoins which is more then one thousand dollars!

Locker ransomware and crypto ransomware

First, let's talk about locker ransomware. Locker ransomware locks you out forcing you to pay so you can gain control over your device.
Crypto ransomware encrypts your confidential and important files. They will threaten to destroy your files unless you pay a fee.

How can ransomware get to you?

The three main ways ransomware can get to you is..
Spam Emails
Spear phishing
Water hole attacks
Scareware tactics are also another way to spread ransomware by creating fake software or messages. They looks believable but demands you pay to get your device back.

How can you fight against a ransomware?

Well you need to recognise if it's safe or not. Don't open any suspicious emails and links. Here's a tip, if you look at a link and it has "https", then it's safe because the s in htpps means secured. If it doesn't have a s then don't click on it. Be sure to back up your device and always use up-to-date internet software with anti-ransomware technologies. Never pay a ransom as it encourages the cyber criminal and continue doing it.
